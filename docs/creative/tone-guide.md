# ChronoQueue — Tone Guide

This document defines the voice of ChronoQueue. It's the reference for anyone writing quest text, item descriptions, UI copy, combat logs, or system messages. The Game Designer and Art Director should treat this as a binding constraint, not a suggestion.

---

## The North Star

**"A DM who takes the rules seriously but not the fiction."**

The systems are mechanically honest — damage numbers mean something, progression curves are balanced, item stats matter. But the world they describe is absurd — your tank is named Gerald and he keeps getting set on fire, the quest to retrieve the Sacred Chalice leads to a breakroom argument with a lich about whose turn it is to wash the dishes.

The humor comes from the *gap* between the mechanical rigor and the narrative absurdity. The game is serious about being a game. It is not serious about being an epic fantasy.

---

## Reference Touchstones

| Reference | What We Take From It |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Progress Quest** | The origin. "The game plays itself" as a premise, not just a punchline. We inherit the concept and add real choices. |
| **Kingdom of Loathing** | Humor-first RPG with genuine mechanical depth. Closest tonal cousin. Items are jokes, but they have real stats. |
| **Dwarf Fortress** (adventure logs) | Emergent narrative through dry, systemic output. "Urist McFighter punches the goblin in the left thumb. The goblin's left thumb is mangled beyond recognition." |
| **Discworld** (Terry Pratchett) | Satirizes fantasy tropes while being a genuinely great fantasy world. The parody IS the worldbuilding. |
| **The Stanley Parable** | Meta-awareness of game conventions without being smug. Knows it's a game. Doesn't make that the whole personality. |
| **Hades** (narrator voice) | Warm, witty, character-driven narration. Makes you feel like you're in on the joke with the storyteller. |

---

## What We Are NOT

- **Not "lol random."** Every joke should have structure. "Spork of Doom" is lazy. "Sword of Adequate Sharpness (+3, occasionally +4 when it feels like it)" has craft.
- **Not ironic detachment.** We genuinely love RPGs. The parody comes from deep knowledge, not contempt.
- **Not cringe humor.** No toilet jokes, no shock value, no "so bad it's good." The humor is dry, observational, and rewards genre literacy.
- **Not a meme delivery system.** No internet references that will age in six months. The humor should work in 2030 the same as it does today.
- **Not mean-spirited.** We laugh *with* the tropes, not *at* the players who enjoy them. A player of traditional RPGs should feel seen, not mocked.

## Voice Guidelines by Context

### Quest Text
**Tone:** Understated and dry. The narrator is filing a report, not performing a comedy set. Humor comes from juxtaposition — mundane tone describing absurd events.

- YES: "Retrieve the Orb of Sufficient Importance from the cave. The cave's current occupant has been notified and is, reportedly, 'not thrilled.'"
- YES: "Escort the merchant to the next town. The merchant insists on stopping every 200 meters to check if his cart is still there. It is. It always is."
- NO: "OMG go get the CRAZY ORB from the WACKY CAVE lol!!!"
- NO: "This quest is LITERALLY impossible haha good luck!!!"

### Item Descriptions
**Tone:** Functional first, funny second. Every item should read as a real game item that happens to have personality. The stats are real. The flavor is bonus.

- YES: "Iron Helmet of the Perpetually Surprised — +12 DEF. The visor is stuck in the 'up' position, giving the wearer an expression of constant astonishment. Popular at parties."
- YES: "Threadbare Cloak of Invisibility — +2 DEF, +5 Stealth (in dim lighting only). Works great in the dark. Less effective at noon. Completely useless at job interviews."
- NO: "Funny Hat lmao — does nothing but is hilarious"
- NO: "LEGENDARY SWORD OF ULTIMATE DESTRUCTION — this sword is, like, super powerful or whatever"

### Combat Logs
**Tone:** Deadpan reportage. The log doesn't know it's funny. It's a system recording events with clinical precision. The humor emerges from what the system reports, not from the system trying to be funny.

- YES: "Gerald (Lv. 14 Knight) was set on fire by Goblin Pyromancer. Gerald extinguished himself. Gerald was set on fire again. Gerald is beginning to take this personally."
- YES: "Mira (Lv. 8 Ranger) fired an arrow at Skeleton Archer. Miss. Skeleton Archer fired an arrow at Mira. Also miss. Both parties stared at each other for an uncomfortable moment."
- NO: "EPIC FIRE ATTACK!! Gerald is BURNINATING!!! XD"
- NO: "Mira's arrow goes WHOOOOSH and misses by a mile ROFL"

### UI Copy
**Tone:** Clean, professional, slightly knowing. The UI is competent and helpful. It's good at its job. It occasionally lets slip that it knows exactly what kind of game this is.

- YES: "Your party has been idle for 6 hours. They've been busy. [View Log]"
- YES: "Inventory full. Gerald is carrying 47 swords. This seems excessive, but it's your call."
- YES: "New zone discovered: The Mildly Forboding Marshlands."
- NO: "WOW your heroes did SO MUCH STUFF while you were gone!!!!"
- NO: "Oopsie! Too many items! XD"

### System Messages
**Tone:** Efficient and clear. Humor is optional here — clarity always wins. When humor does appear, it's a brief aside, never at the expense of comprehension.

- YES: "Save complete. Your progress is safe. (Gerald's dignity, less so.)"
- YES: "Connection restored. Your party continued without you. They didn't seem to notice."
- NO: "SAVED!!! Your heroes are TOTALLY SAFE now haha"

## The Humor Spectrum

Not every piece of text needs to be funny. The ratio matters.

| Content Type | Humor Frequency | Notes |
|--------------|----------------|-------|
| Quest text | High (70-80%) | This is where the voice lives. Most quests should have personality. |
| Item descriptions | Medium-high (60-70%) | Functional items can be straight. Named/rare items should have flavor. |
| Combat logs | Medium (40-50%) | Most log entries are functional. Occasional dry humor elevates key moments. |
| UI copy | Low-medium (20-30%) | Mostly clean and professional. Rare knowing winks. |
| System messages | Low (10-15%) | Clarity first. Humor only when it costs nothing. |
| Tutorials/help | Very low (5%) | Teach first. A single well-placed joke per tutorial section, max. |

## Writing Principles

1. **Understatement > overstatement.** "Gerald has had better days" hits harder than "Gerald is having the WORST DAY EVER!!!!"
2. **Specificity is comedy.** "A sword" isn't funny. "A slightly bent sword with someone else's name engraved on it" is.
3. **The system doesn't know it's funny.** The combat log, the quest board, the inventory — they're all doing their jobs. The humor is emergent, not performed.
4. **Earn escalation.** Start dry. Get weirder gradually. The player should think "wait, did that really just happen?" not "oh, another random joke."
5. **Reward re-reading.** The best lines should be funnier the second time because the player now understands the systems well enough to appreciate the subtext.
6. **Respect the player's intelligence.** No explaining the joke. No "get it?" No winking at the camera. Trust the reader.
